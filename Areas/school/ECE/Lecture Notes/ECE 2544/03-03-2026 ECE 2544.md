### Shift Operations 


There are three types of shift operations 
- Logical Shift
- Circular shift (or rotate)
- Arithmetic shift


#### Logical shift

- The bits added are 0's 
- if i shifting to the right all bits who overflow right are removed and in the left 0's are pushed in


#### Circular shift
- The bit that overflows during shifting is pushed into the back of the sequence
- If shifting right the bit overflowing on the right should now be the MSB 

#### Arithmetic Shift
- We take the register contents to represent a signed 2's complement binary number 
	- Left shift corresponds to multiplication by 2
	- right shift corresponds to division by 2

Practically speaking, this means:
- An arithmetic-shift left is a **logical shift left**, as long as overflow does not occur.
- An arithmetic shift right uses the current sign bit to occupy the **vacated position**

- when doing these shift operations one must keep in mind the range of the sequence of bits eg how many numbers can a 5 bit 2s complement sequence represent.
