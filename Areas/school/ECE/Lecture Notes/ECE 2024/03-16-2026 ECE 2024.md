- Noise occurs everywhere in nature 
- we use filters to remove as much as we can

- Where does noise come from 
	- Thermal agitation
	- Shot noise

- As we learned before we learned that impedance is a function of frequency.

$$
\begin{align}
Z_{L} = j2\pi fL, &  & Z_{c}=-\frac{j}{2\pi fC} 
\end{align}
$$

- For a DC signal (zero frequency)
	- $Z_{L}=0$ and $Z_{c}=\infty$
- For a signal with infinite frequency 
	- $Z_{L}=\infty$ and $Z_{c}=0$

- This property lets us create filters using inductors and capacitors as the in-between condition so where the frequency isnt infinity or 0 changes what frequency is passed. 


## Circuits as filters

**Two port network:**
- We can look at any system as a two port network:
- The input connects a signal at a given frequency to a load at the output port.
- The percentage of the input signal delivered to the load depends on the "frequency response" of the two-port network.
- The percentage of the input signal delivered to the load depends on the "frequency response" of the two port network.

**Transfer function definition**
- It is the ratio of the input-to-output signal as a function of frequency. 
$$
H(f)=\frac{V_{out}\angle\theta_{out}}{V_{in}\angle\theta_{in}} 
$$

### First Order Low Pass filter
- This circuit trys to pass low frequencies hence the name low pass 
- Capacitor in parallel with the load will pass low frequencies  

![[Pasted image 20260316114826.png#invert|400]]
- Equation for $V_{out}$ is given as 
$$
V_{out} = V_{in} \frac{Z_{c}}{R+Z_{c}}=V_{in} \frac{\frac{1}{j2\pi fC}}{R+\frac{1}{j 2\pi fC}}
=V_{in} \frac{1}{1+j 2\pi fRC}
$$
- Solving this we simply started with a voltage divider and solved using existing impedance equations.  


**Break frequency**
$$
f_{b} = \frac{1}{2 \pi RC}
$$
> this is also called the half-power frequency 




## Bode Plots
- The transfer function gives two outputs 
	- The magnitude of the transfer functions shows how the amplitude of each frequency component is affected by the filter. 
	- The phase of the transfer function shows how the phase of each frequency component is affected by the filter it represents. 
- bode plots are always in pairs one for magnitude and one for phase. 


### High Pass filters
$$
H(f)=\frac{j\frac{f}{f_{b}}}{1+j \frac{f}{f_{b}}}
$$
![[Pasted image 20260318113222.png#invert|center|400]]


- at low frequencies the inductor is a short 
- as the frequency increases the inductor will look like an open circuit relative to the high frequency 
$$
\begin{align}
f_{b}=\frac{1}{2\pi RC}  &  &  f_{b}=\frac{1}{2\pi j \frac{f}{f_{b}}}
\end{align}
$$
> for RC filters and RL filters respectively 



### Decibel

- logarithmic x 
$$|H(f)|_{db}=10\log|\frac{v_{out}}{v_{in}}|^2=20\log| \frac{V_{out}}{V_{in}}|$$
- db means the change in the input relative to the output
