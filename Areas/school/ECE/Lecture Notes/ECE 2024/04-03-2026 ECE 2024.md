## Diodes 
- A diode functions like a faucet when you apply a voltage with teh correct polarity it lets the current flow.
- An **electron hole** is the absence of an electron at a position where an electron could exist in an atom.
	- it leaves a hole in the latus structure of the material and leaves a positive charge. 

**P-type doping**
- adds holes to the semiconductor 

**N-type doping** 
- Adds the electrons to the semiconductor 

- The electrons don't actually just move through a circuit but rather collide with one another in such a way where they move on net through the circuit 

- As a result of trapped charge in the junction we get our voltage drop across the diode

- On condition is called **Forward biasing** 
- The field created by a battery across a diode is opposite to the field across the junction and they cancel this then shrinks the junction and allows electrons to flow.
	- Because the field in the junction is fixed by the amount of trapped electrons in the junction we always get .7 voltage drop across an active diode. 

- Off condition is called **Reverse Biasing**
	- Positive to positive relative to battery and diode 
	- The applied voltage source strengthens the internal electric field the positive terminal is connected to the negative n type cathode 
	- this causes the junction to become wider which prevents current from flowing since the internal electric field is now even stronger.

### Shockley Diode 
$$
\large i_{D}=I_{s}[e^{(\frac {v_{D}} {nV_{T}})}-1]
$$
> $i_{D}$ and $V_{d}$ are the diode current and voltage
> $i_{s}$ is the Reverse Saturation Current
> $V_{t}$ is the Thermal voltage $0.026$ at room temp 
> $n=$ emission coefficient has a value between 1 and 2 is meant to account for imperfections in manufacturing. (when $n=1$ we have an ideal diode)


- If we solve for voltage we get 
$$
v_{d}=nV_{T}\ln\left( \left[ \frac{i_{d}}{I_{s}}  +1 \right] \right)
$$
> Diode equation for Reverse zero and forward
> $I_{s}$ is the leakage current 

$$\LARGE
\begin{align}
\mathrm{Reverse \space Bias:\space}  &  i_{d}=I_{s}[e^{(\frac {v_{D}} {nV_{T}})}-1]   \cong I_{s}[0-1]   \cong -I_{s} \\
\mathrm{Zero \space Bias:\space}   &  
  i_{d}=I_{s}[e^{(\frac {v_{D}} {nV_{T}})}-1]  
 \cong I_{s}[1-1]   \cong 0 \\
\mathrm{Forward \space Bias:\space}   &  
  i_{d}=I_{s}[e^{(\frac {v_{D}} {nV_{T}})}-1]    
\cong I_{s}e^{(\frac{v_{D}}{nV_{T}})}

\end{align}
$$
