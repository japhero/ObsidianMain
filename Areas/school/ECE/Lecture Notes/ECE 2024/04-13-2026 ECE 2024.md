## Schottky and Zener Diodes

- Schottky diodes 
	- Much faster at turn off 
	- Reverse current is way higher 
	- $V_{f}=.3V$ instead of the ususal .7


### Zener Diodes
- Inexpensive regulator 
	- can provide a constant voltage despite a changing load 
- Large reverse bias voltage causes the voltage barrier to "break down" and current is allowed to flow in the reverse direction 
- Normal break down occurs at > 5.6 V and is called the avalanche breakdown effect where enough energy is present to free electron hole pairs below 5.6 volts the zener break down effect occurs 
- Zener diodes have a specific breakdown voltage in which they can continuously operate in the reverse bias mode.
- When the applied voltage varies the **internal impedance compensates to keep the voltage constant.** 




**Applications**
- voltage regulators as mentioned
- just like with other diodes we can use load line to find the operating point 


### Ideal Mode Diode 

- Given its assumed functionality, an ideal diode can be modeled as an on/off switch.
- When the voltage across the diode is less than zero
- (reverse bias) the diode is an open circuit (off position) When the voltage across the diode is zero or greater
- (forward bias) the diode is a short circuit (on position)

### Piece-Wise linear Model 
- take the diode characteristic graph linearize it for different sections and then depending on the load use the given linearized functionality
