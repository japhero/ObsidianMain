# Lecture Notes Transients



- For circuits with a DC source our solution has an added contant component to the transient response 
$$
x(t)_{\text{DC source}} = K_{1} + K_{2}e^-\frac{t}{\tau}
$$

- The steady state response is the response of the circuit after the transient goes to zero. 


- For a general solution, the differential equation is of the form 
$$
\tau\frac{dx(t)}{dt}+x(t)= f(t)
$$

- For most sources, the particular solution will include terms of the function of the source, and its derivative:
$$
A \cdot f(t) \text{ and } B \cdot \frac{df(t)}{dt}
$$
