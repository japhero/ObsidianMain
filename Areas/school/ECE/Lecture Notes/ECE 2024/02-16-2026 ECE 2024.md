# Lecture Notes Responses of Second Order Circuits 


- The roots of the system tell us things about the system 
	- The response to change in the system 
	- They are frequently termed eigenvalues 


$$  \large x(t)=
e^ {-\frac{R}{2L} t} \left(K_{1}e^{t\sqrt{  (\frac R {2L})^2 - \frac 1 {LC}}}  + K_{2}e^{-t\sqrt{  (\frac R {2L})^2 - \frac 1 {LC}}} \right) \tag 1
$$
- The leading exponent $-\frac{R}{2L}$ is called the damping coefficient $\alpha$.
	- It is called this because it dampens the oscillations between cap and inductor energy. 

- This oscillation is sometimes called ringing 
- The remaining sum inside of the parentheses gives the values for the ringing the effect of the leading exponent $e^{-\frac{R}{2L}t}$ simply dampens the effect.

- The dampening depends on the energy dissipated by the resistor. This energy is a function of the current, and the inductor controls the currents behavior in a series circuit. 



## Response cases of RLC circuits 
- The value of the exponents inside the parentheses can be simplified in equation 1 


#### **Case: 1**
$$
\frac{R}{2L}> \frac{1}{\sqrt{ LC }}
$$
- Two different roots both **<u>negative real roots </u>**

- **Overdamped case** because both terms are (non-zero) damping components for $x(t)$



#### **Case 2:**
$$
\frac{R}{2L} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{ LC }}
$$
$$
x(t) = Ke^{\frac{-R}{2L}t}(K_{1}t+K_{2})
$$

- Repeated negative real root for both terms so i can be factored out 

- We called this case the Critically-Damped case because it has the minimum damping required to avoid any energy oscillation



#### **Case 3:** 

$$
\frac{R}{2L} < \frac{1}{\sqrt{ LC }}
$$
- Complex imaginary root $j$ not $i$ because $i$ is already current 

$$
x(t)=e^{-\frac{R}{2L}t}(K_{1}\cos(\omega t)+K_{2}\sin(\omega t))
$$

- We will call this case the Underdamped Case. The relatively low value of R results in low damping (low energy dissipation) that results in a transitory energy oscillation between the capacitor and the inductor.


Resonate frequency $\omega_{d}$  
$$
\omega_{d}= \sqrt{    \frac{1}{ LC }-\left(  \frac{R}{2L}  \right)^2}
$$
- Resonant frequency is the natural reponse rate of the system 



#### **Case 4:**
$$
R = 0
$$
No dampening the system oscillates forever
$$
\omega_{0}= \frac{1}{\sqrt{ LC }}
$$

#### Damping ratio

$$
\zeta = \frac{\alpha}{\omega_{0}} = \frac{\frac{R}{2L}}{\frac{1}{\sqrt{ LC }}}
$$
- This can give us the 3 cases

1. If $\alpha>\omega_{0}$ then $\zeta>1$ the circuit is overdamped 
2. If $\alpha = \omega_{0}$ then $\zeta = 1$  the circuit is critically damped 
3. If $\alpha<\omega_{0}$ then $\zeta < 1$ the circuit is underdamped
