# Lecture Notes Second order Resonance and Filtering


- You can only have low and high pass in first order circuits 

- Second order filters have a resonant frequency 
$$
\omega_{0}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{ LC }}
$$
- In HZ this is 
$$
f_{0}=\frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{ LC }}
$$
- at the resonant frequency the magnitude of the impedance of the cap is equal to the magnitude of the impedance of the inductor. Since they have opposite signs for this the circuit looks purely resistive. 


## Series cases for filtering 
 - we can change the observed output to change the effect on the signal 

**Summary**
1. Low pass filters: Load connected in parallel to the cap. 
2. High pass filters: Load connected in parallel to the inductor. 
3. Band pass filtering: Load connected in parallel to the resistor 
4. Band pass reject Filtering: Load connected in parallel to the series connection of inductor and capacitor. This connection rejects signals around the resonant frequency.

## Effect of impedance to resistance ratio on RLC filters
- At the resonant frequency the impedance of the capacitor equals the impedance of the inductor 

$$
|2\pi f_{0}L|=\frac{|1}{2\pi f_{0}C}| 
$$
- in a series RLC circuit the quality factor is the ratio between the inductor impedance and the resistance at the resonant frequency is known as the quality factor.
$$
Q_{s} = \frac{2\pi f_{0}L}{R}
$$
- Dampening ratio is related to quality factor 
$$
Q_{s}=\frac{\omega_{0}L}{R} = \frac{\omega_{0}}{2\alpha} = \frac{.5}{\zeta}
$$
- When $Q_{s}$ is less than .5 the circuit is overdamped
- When $Q_{S}$ is equal to .5 the circuit is Critically damped 
- When $Q_{s}$ is greater than .5 the circuit is underdamped.
