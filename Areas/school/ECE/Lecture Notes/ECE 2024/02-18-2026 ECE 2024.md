# Lecture Notes Second order RLC in parallel 
- in parallel circuits the capacitor voltage ends up at zero because the inductor shorts out the capacitor under DC conditions. 


The differential is only slightly different

$$
\frac{d^2i_{L}(t)}{dt^2} + \frac{1}{RC} \frac{di_{L}(t)}{dt}+\frac{1}{LC} i_{L}(t)=\frac{1}{LC}I_{s}(t)
$$
> $\frac{R}{L}$ becomes $\frac{1}{RC}$ compared to series circuits
> if we want voltage simply exchange all the $i_{L}(t)$ terms with $v_{C}(t)$ terms


- The important thing here is that in parallel circuits the cap voltage must equal the inductor voltage because they are in series
