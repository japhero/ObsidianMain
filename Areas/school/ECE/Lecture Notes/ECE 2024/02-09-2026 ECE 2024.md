# Lecture Notes Charging Circuits 



## CAPS
- When we connect a source ot the circuit a current $i_{c}$ will flow through the cap 

- if we perform [[Kirchhoff's Laws#Kirchhoff's Voltage Law|KVL]] we get 
$$
-V_{s}+i_{c}R+v_{c}(t)=0
$$
- where 
$$
i_{c}(t)=C \frac{d\, v_{c}(t)}{dt}
$$
- this is a first order ODE and 

$$
RC \frac{d \, v_{c}(t)}{dt} + v_{c}(t) = V_{s}(t)
$$
**CHARGE EQUATION**
$$\LARGE
v_{c} = V_{s} + (v_{c} (0 ^+)-V_{s})e^{\frac{-1}{RC}t}
$$





## INDUCTORS

- using the same properties ideas as with caps and doing KVL we get
$$
\frac{L}{R} \frac{di_{L}(t)}{dt} +i_{L}(t)=\frac{V_{s}(t)}{R}
$$

- we can again solve for the general charge equation for an inductor giving 
$$\LARGE
i_{L}(t)=\frac{V_{s}}{R}+\left( i_{L}(0^{+})-\frac{V_{s}}{R} \right) e^{\frac {-R} {L}t}
$$

- These equations give the obvious solution to DC state considering as t goes to infinity 
- $\displaystyle \lim_{ t \to \infty }(i_{L})=\frac{V_{s}}{R}$ this happens because negative exponentials allways go to zero as they go to infinity 





## BOTH 

- The steady state response is the circuit response to the application of the source after the transient dissipates.  Its the response due to the applied DC source (new steady state)

- The transient is the difference between the steady state and state of either the cap or the inductor when it is charging. Rather the transient describes what the cap or inductor is doing as it charges or discharges.
