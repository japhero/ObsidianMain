# Afton explanation
- The idea that two waves can pass through each other and they can effect each other while both waves exist 
	- Its constructive when their amplitudes add and destructive when the subtract 



# ChatGPT quick guide 

When two ore more waves overlap the resulting displacement is the algabraic sum:
$$
y_{total} = y_{1}(x,t) +y_{2}(x,t)
$$


## constructive interference 

- Amplitudes add 

$$
A_{result} = A_{1} +A_{2}
$$
- Phase condiiton
$$
\Delta \phi = 0,2\pi,4\pi\dots
$$

- Path difference 

$$
\Delta r=m\lambda
$$

## Destructive interference 
- Waves are out of phase 
- Crest + trough 
- Amplitudes subtract

$$A_{result}= |A_{1}-A_{2}|$$

Phase condition
$$
\Delta \phi = \pi,3\pi\dots
$$

Path difference
$$
\Delta r = \left( m+\frac{1}{2} \right) \lambda
$$

## Phase difference 
$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta r
$$
> $\Delta r$ = path difference 
> $\lambda$ = wavelength



## standing waves 
- Two identical waves traveling in opposite direcitons

- nodes allways have zero displacement due to distructive interfernece
- antinodes have max displacement 

## string fixed at both ends 

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda}{2}, n=1,2,3\dots
$$
## beats
- two waves close frequencies same medium

$f_{beat}=| f_{1}-f_{2}|$


## Path difference 
**Path difference** is the **difference in distance** that two waves travel from their sources to the same point in space.

$$
\Delta r= | r_{2}-r_{1}|
$$
> $r_{1},r_{2}$: distances from each source to the point 


## boundary conditions

- strings, electromagnetic waves, and sound displacement waves in closed-closed tubes must have nodes at both ends:
$$
\lambda_{m} = \frac{2L}{m}, f_{m}=m \frac{v}{2L}=mf_{1}, m = 1,2,3
$$

The frequencies and wavelengths are the same for a sound wave in an open-open tube, which has antinodes at both ends.

A sound displacement wave in an open-closed tube must have a node at the closed end but an antinode at the open end. This leads to

$\lambda_{m}=\frac{4L}{m},f_{m}=m \frac{v}{4L}=mf_{1},m=1,3,5,7\dots$
