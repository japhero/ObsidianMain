# DUMP Chapter 24 Gauss stuff/Electric Flux





> [!Definition] Gauss's law
>  
> 
> Electric flux through any closed surface depends only on net charge inside that surface.
>> 





## Surfaces

For any closed surface enclosing total charge $\mathrm{Q_{encl.}}$ the net electric flux through the surface is:
$$
\Phi_{E}= \oint \vec{E} \cdot d \vec{A}=\frac{\mathrm{Q_{encl}}}{\epsilon_{0}} 
$$

- What if the charge within the shape is not centered.
	- the size of the surface doesn't matter
	- the location of the charge doesn't matter




## Conductors Within gauss's law 


- within a conductor during equilibrium $\vec{E}=0$ 
	- This happens because the electrons can move within the conductor and then will concentrate on one side of the conductor which in turn polarizes it causing equilibrium
