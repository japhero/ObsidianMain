## Electric forces  Chapter 22



- Universal principle of charge conservation sates that the sum of all the electric charges in any closed system is constant.
$$
q=N_{p}e-N_{e}e=\text{constant}
$$

### Electric Charge

- Charge of a proton 
$$q_{p}=1.6 \cdot 10^{-19} C = e$$
- Charge of a electron 
$$
q_{e}=-1.6 \cdot 10^{-19} = -e
$$

- Charge on any object can be expressed by the equation
$$
\begin{align}
q & =N_{p}e+N_{e}(-e) \\
 & =(N_{p}-N_{e})e
\end{align}


$$
> where $N_{p}$ and $N_{e}$ are the number of protons and electrons respectively



### Coulombs Law

![[coulombs Law 2-10-26 PHYS|center]]

$$
|\vec{F}_{1} | = | \vec{F}_{2} | =F = \frac{k | q_{1 } q_{2} |}{r^2}
$$
> $k:$ electric constant $= 9 \times 10^9 \frac{\,Nm^2}{C^2}$
> $k$ can be written as $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}$ where $\epsilon_{0}=8.85 \times 10^{-12} \, \frac{C^2}{nm^2}$



###  Electric Field $(\vec{E})$

- Every charge is the source of an electric field
- We can relate this concept to gravity because every mass i a source of gravitational field 

![[Electric Filed example 02-10-26 PHYS|center|600]]
- Understanding this we can represent the force of one field on the other electron 
$$
\text{Force on $q_2$ is } q_{2} \times \text{ Field of $q_{1}$}
$$

### Uniform $\vec{E}$ field 
- Unlike point charges the effect on a test charge within an electric field is uniform  


![[Uniform E field Diagram 2-12-2026 PHYS|center]]



**Equations**
- We can use general [[Physics Motion Models#Constant Acceleration Kinematic equations|kinematic equations]] to define the behavior of charges considering $\vec{F} =m \vec{a}$ and $\vec{F}=q_{0} \vec{E}$

we can solve for acceleration
$$
|\vec{a}| = \frac{| q_{0} \vec{E} |}{m}
$$


- using kinematic equations we can then solve for things like velocity as normal giving 
$$
v_{f} = \sqrt{ 2ad }
$$

### Equations for Electric Fields


**Small Positive Source**

$$
\vec{E} = \frac{kq}{r^2}
$$
**Small Negative Source**
$$
\vec{E}=\frac{kq}{r^2}
$$
**Positive source spherical charge**
$$
\vec{E}= \frac{kQ}{r^2}
$$
- Inside the sphere the charge is zero

**Two parallel plates**
$$
\vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_{0}}
$$
