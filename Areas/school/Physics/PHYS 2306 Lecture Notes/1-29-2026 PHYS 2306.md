## Traveling Waves 
- Wave that is never reflected 
- all mechanical waves need a medium
	- This includes all traveling waves except for light.


- Transverse is a wave that oscillates perpendicular to the direction of the waves advance.
- Longitudinal wave is a wave were the particles are displaced in the same direction as the direction of travel for the wave.

![[PHYS 2306 Wave Description]]
- With string, the medium effects the speed of the wave 
- we can express the speed of the string with the expression
$$
v = \sqrt{ \frac{F_{\tau}}{\mu}}
$$

> $\mu$ is the $\frac{kg}{m}$ of the string

also
$$
v = \frac{\lambda}{T} = \lambda f
$$

- When the energy from the string is transferred into the air the frequency doesnt change but the wavelength changes to compensate for the change in speed. 


## The Doppler Shift
- Your motion will create a shift in the frequency 
	- If moving away from the source the frequency relative to the movement will be lower.
	- If moving towards the source the relative frequency will be higher. 

$$
v= f \lambda
$$
![[PHYS 2306 Doppler effect example]]
- the motion of the observer and the motion of the source dont have the same effect. 

- When the source is moving
$$
f=f_{0}\left( \frac{V}{V \pm V_{S}} \right)
$$
> Add $V_{s}$ when the source is towards the source 


- When the listener is moving 
$$
f=f_{0}\left( \frac{V\pm V_{L}}{V} \right)
$$
> you add $V_{L}$ when the listener is moving away from the source
