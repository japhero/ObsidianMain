### Newtons Law of Cooling 


$\theta$ is the temperature of the object
$s$ is the temperature of the surroundings


$$
\theta (t)^` = k(\theta(t)-s(t))
$$

Ex: A metal object is heated to $200^\circ C$ and then is placed in a large room to cool down. The room temperature is held at $20 ^\circ C$. After 10 minutes the objects temperature is $100^\circ C$. 

(a) How long will it take to reach $25^\circ C$. 


$$
\begin{array}{l}
\frac{d\theta}{dt}  = k(20-\theta)  \\
 \int \frac{d\theta}{20-\theta}   = \int k \, dt  \\
\ln(\theta-20)=-kt+C \\
\theta-20=De^{-kt} \\
\end{array}
$$
- plug in initial value to find $D$
$$
\theta  = 20 +180e^{-kt}
$$
