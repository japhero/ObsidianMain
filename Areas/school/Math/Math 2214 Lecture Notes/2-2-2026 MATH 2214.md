## Section 2.4 Population Models

**Simple model for population**

- we treat the population growth as proportional to the current population
$$
\frac{dP}{dt}=kP, \, P(0)=P_{0}
$$
- Solving the differential equation we get 
$$
P=P_{0}e^{kt}
$$

EXAMPLE
An aquaculture firm raises catfish in ponds. At the beginning of the year the ponds contain approximately $500,000$ catfish. The net growth is $6.1/1000$ per week. The firm wants to harvest at a constant rate $R$ fish per week, but it also wants to increase the population to about $600,000$ by the end of the year. Find the harvesting rate $R$. 

$$
\begin{array}{ll}
P(0) = 500,000 \\
P(52) = 600,000 \\
\frac{dP}{dt}=.00061P-R \\ \\

 \underline{\displaystyle \frac{dP}{dt}-.00061P=-R}
\end{array}
$$

- Simple seperation of variables

$$
\begin{array}{\ll}
\frac{dQ}{dt}=-kQ \\
\int \frac{dQ}{Q}=\int -k \, dt \\
\ln (Q)=-kt+C \\
Q=De^{-kt}  & Q_{0}=D \\
Q=Q_{0}e^{-kt}
\end{array}
$$



**EXAMPLE**

Initially $50mg$ of a radioactive substance is present. Five days later the quantity has decreased to $43mg$ how much will remain after 30 days. 
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
Q=50e^{-kt} \\
\frac{43}{50}= \cancel{\frac{50}{50}}e^{-5k} \\
\ln\left( \frac{43}{50} \right) =-5k \\
k=-\frac{1}{5}\ln\left( \frac{43}{50} \right)=0.03016 \\
Q=50e^{-.03016t} \\
Q(30)=50e^{(-0.03016)(30)}=20.228mg
\end{array}


$$
