## Definitions

### Section 4.3 Homogenous systems 

- When we write something bold it is a column matrix 


- hence bold y 
$$
\mathbf{y}= \begin{bmatrix}
y_{1} \\
y_{2} \\
\vdots \\
y_{n}
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
\mathbf{y'}=P(t) \mathbf{y} ,\mathbf{y}(t_{0}) = \mathbf{y_{0}} \tag **
$$
If $\mathbf{y_{1},\dots,y_{r}}$ are $r$ solutions of $(*)$ then any linear combination of the solutions defined is also a solution to the system $(c_{1} \mathbf{y_{1}}+\dots+ c_{r} \mathbf{y_{r}})$ 

> [!definition]
> if every solution of $\mathbf{y'}=P(t) \mathbf{y}$ can be written as a linear combination of $\mathbf{y_{1},y_{2},\dots,y_{n}}$ then we call $\mathbf{y_{1},y_{2},\dots,y_{n}}$ a fundamental set of solutions.

#### Ables theorem for matrices

Let $y_{1},y_{2},..,y_{n}$ be a set of solutions of $y'=p(t)y (a<t<b)$ then 
$$
\begin{align}
W'(t)= & \mathrm{tr}[P(t)]W(t) \\
W(t)= & W(t_{0})e^{\int_{t_{0}}^t \mathrm{tr[P(s)]} \, ds }
\end{align}
$$
> where $\mathrm{tr[f(t)]}$ is the trace of the function meaning the sum of the elements on its main diagonal
