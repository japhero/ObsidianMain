# Section 2.10 Euler's method 

- Consider this first order differential equation

$$

\begin{array}{ll}
y'=f(t,y)  & y(t_{0})=y_{0}
\end{array}
$$
- If there are points close to the initial value problem we try and find their values. 
	- Eulers method is an linear approximation.

![[Eulers aproximation]]
- Linear approximation using the slope given by the initial condition 

- using point slope 
$$
y_{k+1}=y_{k}+f(t_{k},y_{k})(t_{k+1}-t_{k})
$$
> this formula is recursive and must be used in sequence to find distances of more than one to the known point. 

## Examples 


Given 
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
y'=t^2+y & y(2)=1 & t_{1}=2.1,t_{2}=2.2,t_{3}=2.3
\end{array}
$$
find $y_{1},y_{2},y_{3}$



$$
\begin{array}{ll}
 y_{1} & =y_{0}+f(t_{0},y_{0})(t_{1}-t_{0}) \\
 & =y(2)+(2^2+1)(2.1-2) \\
 & =1+(5(.1)) \\
y_{1} & =1.5
\end{array}
$$
Then repeat these steps with our now given value of $y_{1}$ to find $y_{2}$ and then use that value to find $y_{3}$ respectively.
