# Lecture Notes Section 3.6 Continued 



- For fixed mass systems the dampening is proportional to the velocity so
$$
\begin{align}
my''+\gamma y'+ky=0
\end{align}
$$
- This is basically just a modified version of the previously described second order homogenous differential 



> [!Example]- A block weighing 8 pounds is attached to the end of a spring causing the spring to stretch 6 inches beyond its natural length. The block is then pulled 3 inches and released. Determine the motion of the block. Assuming there is no dampening.
>  
> 
> $$
> \begin{array}{ll}
> m y''+ky=0 
> \end{array}
> $$
> 
> We divide weight by $g$ to get the mass. We stay in American units so 
> 
> $$
> \begin{array}{ll}
> 8/32=\frac{1}{4} \mathrm{\space slog}, & k=\frac{8}{\frac{6}{12}}=16 \mathrm{\space lb/ft}
> \end{array}
> $$
> > $k$ is simply hooks law where we convert the 6 inches to the proper unit feet
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> 0 & =\frac{1}{4} y''+ 16y,  & y_{0}=\frac{1}{4},  &  & y'(0)=0 
> \end{align}
> $$
> > we get $y_{0} \text{ and } y_{0}'$ from initial problem statement
> 
> 
> characteristic polynomial
> $$
> \begin{align}
>  & \lambda^2+64=0 \\
>  & \lambda= \pm 8
> \end{align}
> $$
> Plug into solution
> 
> $$
> \begin{align}
> y & =A\cos(8t)+B\sin(8t) \\
>  y' & =-8A\sin(8t)+8B\cos(8t) \\ \\
> 
> y(0) & =  A=\frac{1}{4} \\  
> A & = \frac{1}{4} \\ \\  
> 
> y'(0) & =8B=0 \\
> B & =0
> \end{align}
> $$
> 
> **Now we get the final solution**
> $$
> \begin{align}
> y(t) & =\frac{1}{4}\cos(8t)+(0)\sin(8t) \\
>  & =\boxed{\frac{1}{4}\cos(8t)}
> \end{align}
> $$
