## Section 4.4 Finding $y_{1},y_{2},\dots,y_{n}$ given a coefficient matrix

**Constant coefficient homogenous equations**

$$
y'=A\mathbf{y}, (-\infty<t<\infty)
$$
- The general solution is 
$$
c_{1}y_{1}+c_{2}y_{2}+\dots+c_{n}y_{n}
$$
> where $y_{1},y_{2},\dots,y_{n}$ forms a fundamental set of solutions 

- We create use a guess for the each solution of the form
$$
y=e^{\lambda t} \mathbf{x}
$$
> where $\mathbf{x}$ is a column matrix 

- to prove this we take the derivative
$$
\begin{align}
y & =e^{\lambda t} \mathbf{x} \\
y' & = \lambda e^{\lambda t} \mathbf{x} \\
Ay  &=e^{\lambda t}A \mathbf{x}                                             
\end{align}
$$
if we solve here for $A \mathbf{x}$ we get the equation
$$
A \mathbf{x}= \lambda \mathbf{x}
$$
- This is the eigenvector equation meaning that for the solution we find the eigen value to find lambda.

To find the eigenvalues we take the solution to 
$$
(A-\lambda I)\mathbf{x}=0
$$
- When this equation has a determinant of zero eg $\det[A-\lambda I]=0$ we have several solutions
