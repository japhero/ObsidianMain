# Lecture Notes Section 3.8 The method of undetermined Coefficients 

- The solution to a second order linear non-homogenous equation can be given by 
$$
y(t) = y_{c}(t) +y_{p}(t)
$$
> where $y_{c}(t)$ is called the complementary solution and $y_{p}(t)$ is called the particular solution 



- when we have a right side where taking the derivative doesnt change the function we can take the derivative and plug them into the innital differential equation to solve for coefficents $A$ and $B$ 
	- The functions who which this would work for are $\cos(ct)$ and $e^{ct}$ where $c$ are any coefficient function.
