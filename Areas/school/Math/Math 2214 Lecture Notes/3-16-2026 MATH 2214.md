## Section 3.9 Review 

- Given 
$$
y''+p(t)y'+q(t)y=g(t)

$$
- We want to find a particular solution 
- We assume that 
$$
y''+p(t)y'+g(t)y=0
$$
- we can write the particular solution in the form of
$$
y_{p}=u_{1}y_{1} +u_{2}y_{2}
$$
- if we keep going we get the matrix 
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
y_{1} & y_{2}  \\
y_{1}' & y_{2}'
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
u_{1}' \\
u_{2}' 
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
g
\end{bmatrix}
$$
- Solving we can write this as 
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
u_{1}'  \\
u_{2}'
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
y_{1} & y_{2} \\
y_{1}' & y_{2}'
\end{bmatrix}^{-1}
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
g
\end{bmatrix}
$$
- becasue the 2x2 inverted matrix is the wronskin matrix we know that its determinant must be non zero and therefore it must be invertible. The Wronskian value was given earlier as $y_{1}$ and $y_{2}$ were listed as a fundamental set which makes their Wronskian uninvertible 

- Finally we get 
$$
\begin{align}
u_{1}' & =\frac{y_{2}g}{W} \\
u_{2}' & = \frac{y_{1}g}{W} \\
u_{1} & =\int \frac{-y_{2}g}{W}  \, dt \\
u_{2} & = \int \frac{y_{1}g}{W} \, dt 
\end{align}
$$
Finally we would get 
$$
y_{p} = -y_{1}\int \frac{y_{2}g}{W} \, dt +y_{2} \int \frac{y_{1}g}{W} \, dt 
$$


## Section 3.10

$$
\begin{align}
 & my''+ky=F_{a}(t) \\
 & y''+\frac{k}{m} y  =F\cos(\omega_{i}t) \\
 & y(0)=0, y'(0)=0
\end{align}
$$

- assume that $\frac{k}{m}=\omega_{0}^2$ therefore $\omega_{0}=\sqrt{ \frac{k}{m} }$
$$
\begin{align}
y''+w_{0}^2y=F\cos(\omega_{1}t)
\end{align}
$$
### Case 1 $\omega_{1} \not =\omega_{0}$
$$
y_{c}=C_{1}\sin(\omega t)+C_{2}\cos(\omega_{0}t)
$$
> Complementary solution 

- use the method of undetermined coefficients 

$$
\begin{align}
 & y_{c}=C_{1}\sin(\omega t)+C_{2}\cos(\omega_{0}t) \\
 & y_{p}= A\cos(\omega_{1}t)+B\sin(\omega_{1}t) \\
 & y_{p}'=-A\omega_{1} \sin(\omega_{1}t)+B\omega_{1}  \cos(\omega_{1}t) \\
 & y_{p}''=-A\omega_{1}^2\cos(\omega_{1}t)-B\omega_{1}^2\sin (\omega t)
\end{align}
$$
- plug into innital equation
$$
-A\omega_{1}^2\cos \omega_{1}(t)-B\omega^2_{1}\sin(\omega_{1}t)+A\omega_{0}^2\cos \omega_{1}t+B\omega_{0}^2\sin \omega_{1}^2t=F\cos(\omega_{1}t)
$$
$$
A(\omega_{0}^2-\omega_{1}^2)\cos(\omega_{1}t)+
B(\omega_{0}^2-\omega_{1}^2)\sin(\omega_{1}t)=
F\cos (\omega_{1}t)
$$
$$
\begin{align}
 & A(\omega_{0}^2-\omega_{1}^2)=F  \to &  A=\frac{F}{\omega_{0}^2-\omega_{1}^2}\\
 & B(\omega_{0}^2-\omega_{1}^2)=0 \to  & B=0
\end{align}
$$

- final complementary solution is 
$$
\boxed{y=C_{1}\sin(\omega_{0}t)+C_{2}\cos(\omega_{0}t)+\frac{F}{\omega_{0}^2-\omega_{1}^2}\cos(\omega_{1}t)
}$$
