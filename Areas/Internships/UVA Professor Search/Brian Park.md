### Project Description:
Advanced features in the automobile improve safety and mobility, such as an adaptive cruise control (ACC) system. However, ACC at times does not work well. For example, when the lead vehicle changes its lane, the ego (i.e., the ACC) vehicle takes off to find a new lead vehicle to follow at a preset close distance. Due to this, some drivers do not engage in the ACC. We have developed an algorithm to overcome this limitation and conducted a driving simulator-based experiment. Now, we are to expand it to virtual reality-based evaluation and hardware-in-the-loop (i.e., using a hardware device to control vehicle) based evaluation (before field implementation). In order to do this, a student will work with a team of researchers at the Link Lab and Virginia State University, and be involved in designing and assessing cybersecurity vulnerabilities. Work experience with an embedded Linux OS, and modifying open-source codes and compiling them for implementation are needed.
