# Random Notes 
- This is one of things that i noticed while reading i think i really liked this book when i read it because it is so young adult, it does such a good job encapsulating the experience of a imperfect kid figuring himself and the rest of the world out. When reading some of the internal monolog of pudge it reminds me of an immature version of myself and like i said earlier i think thats why when i read the book the first time i really liked it not because pudge's experiences are extremely relatable but because his thought process was. 





# Review 

Preface this is my review from reading the book for the second time the first time I read this book was in my junior year of HS just like pudge. 

Having read the book before made it much less suspenseful and made the major plot and the countdown style of the first 3/4 of the book much less intriguing, considering i knew the ending from the outset. Although the book stood out to me for the way in which it conveyed pudge and really made me reflect on the myself as when I read this book it reminded me so much of a person who I was like when I read this book the first time. Not that I really had the same struggles as pudge simply the way he interacted with the world around him his internal monolog reminded me so much of the "young teenage kid" that I was. This is why I think i really really liked the book the first time i read it because my proximity to that version of myself was much smaller than it is now. More specifically pudge being an imperfect kid trying to figure out himself and his relationships with others while making mistakes being immature and just doing dumb "teenage kid" things was so relatable to me. Its also why I think I dont remember this book being as much of a romance novel with its admittedly very corny sections that now make me cringe a little. 

**Who should read this**
Recommending this is complicated as I think the people who would like this book the most are people in the same phase of life as the characters mainly HS age students. Although I think this book also makes sense to people who like romance novels as reading the book the second time I realized how much of a component that was. If that's not your thing (it really wasnt mine) this book tells a good HS teenage kid story that isnt exclusively about his romantic endeavors and asks interesting questions about people in general through the character of miles/pudge ESPECIALLY the last third.

I think his relationship with Alaska is interesting and the way he interacts with her throughout the book is extremely indicative of him as a character. More specifically his mannerisms towards her like his objectification of her along with him building a fantasy around her after her death and him eventually wrestling with her legacy and "how to escape the labyrinth" are pointers to his flaws but also how he grows out of them. I wont elaborate on specifics because that would take to long lol but I think overall she works as the main vehicle for his character progression and importantly development.
